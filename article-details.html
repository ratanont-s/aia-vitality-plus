Exercise

Trouble sleeping, poor appetite, feeling stressful? Take a mental wellbeing assessment, earn 100 points and get a free doctor consultation.

Period 1- 15 August 2021

Covid-19 vaccines are relatively new; and even though studies are quite limited to prove whether the vaccination can be more effective on people who engage in regular exercise than those who are physically inactive, some studies have shown that people with active routine develop higher antibodies after vaccination, compared to individuals with high fat levels.

A total of 150-minute work out a week at 20-week consistency can improve the vaccine efficacy. Physical workout can increase antibody levels that functionally detect and fight against viruses and bacteria. Regular exercise also increases numbers of T-cell, an adaptive immune system that targets and eliminates pathogens. It also balances the immune system so the body stimulates stronger immune response to the vaccines.

Physically active lifestyle and regular workout are crucial, as efficient immune system and being healthy help minimize the risk of severe and fatal infections as well as reduce morbidity of Covid-19. Exercise also prevents against NCDs diseases such as obesity, diabetes, respiratory conditions as well as reduces stress and inflammation.

AIA Vitality members earn Vitality points from exercise up to 15,000 points/ membership year from various workouts and daily steps. Simply link your fitness apps or devices with AIA Vitality application and 1,000 Vitality points by submitting proof of receiving COVID-19 vaccination.
